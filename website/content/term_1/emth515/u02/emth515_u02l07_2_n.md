# U02L07 – Combining Integrals and Composite Partitions (Notes)

## Key Results
### 1. RS sum definition
\[
S(P,f,lpha)=\sum f(t_i)\Deltalpha_i.
\]

### 2. Fundamental inequality
\[
L(P,f,lpha)\le S(P,f,lpha)\le U(P,f,lpha).
\]

### 3. Convergence criterion
If RS sums converge to \(A\) as the mesh \(\|P\|	o 0\), then:
\[
\int_a^b f\, dlpha = A,
\]
and \(f \in R(lpha)\).

### 4. Integrability characterization
\[
\sup L(P,f,lpha) = \inf U(P,f,lpha)
\iff
f\in R(lpha).
\]

## Important Expressions
- Lower sum:
  \[
  L(P,f,lpha)=\sum m_i\Deltalpha_i.
  \]
- Upper sum:
  \[
  U(P,f,lpha)=\sum M_i\Deltalpha_i.
  \]

## Example
Discontinuous function
\[
f(x)=1_{\mathbb{Q}}(x)-1_{\mathbb{R}\setminus\mathbb{Q}}(x)
\]
fails integrability since lower sum = \(-1\), upper sum = 1.  
But \( |f|=1 \) is integrable → shows importance of controlling upper–lower gap.

## Summary
U02L07 completes the characterization of Riemann–Stieltjes integrability through RS sums.  
Convergence of sums ⇔ integrability, and the integral equals the limit of composite sums.
