# U02L07 – Combining Integrals and Composite Partitions (Transcript)

## Learning Outcomes
- Understand Riemann–Stieltjes sums as limits of composite sums.
- Prove fundamental inequalities:  
  \[
  L(P,f,lpha)\le S(P,f,lpha)\le U(P,f,lpha).
  \]
- Show that convergence of RS sums implies integrability.
- Characterize the RS integral as the limit of sums when the mesh goes to zero.

## Definition of Riemann–Stieltjes Sum
Given:
- \(f\) bounded on \([a,b]\),
- \(lpha\) monotone increasing on \([a,b]\),
- \(P=\{x_0,\dots,x_n\}\) a partition,
- \(t_i\in[x_{i-1},x_i]\),

the RS sum is:
\[
S(P,f,lpha)=\sum_{i=1}^n f(t_i)\Deltalpha_i,
\quad
\Deltalpha_i = lpha(x_i)-lpha(x_{i-1}).
\]

## Basic Inequality
Let:
\[
m_i=\inf f([x_{i-1},x_i]),\quad
M_i=\sup f([x_{i-1},x_i]).
\]

Then for each \(i\):
\[
m_i\Deltalpha_i \le f(t_i)\Deltalpha_i \le M_i\Deltalpha_i.
\]

Summing gives:
\[
L(P,f,lpha)\le S(P,f,lpha)\le U(P,f,lpha).
\]

## Convergence of RS Sums
A sequence of RS sums \(S(P,f,lpha)\) converges to \(A\) as the mesh \(\|P\|	o 0\) if:
- For every \(arepsilon>0\), there exists \(\delta>0\) such that  
  \[
  |S(P,f,lpha)-A|<arepsilon \quad 	ext{whenever } \|P\|<\delta.
  \]

### Key idea
If RS sums converge to \(A\), then both:
\[
\sup_P L(P,f,lpha) = \inf_P U(P,f,lpha)=A.
\]

This implies:
\[
f\in R(lpha)
\quad	ext{and}\quad
\int_a^b f\,dlpha = A.
\]

### Proof outline from lecture
1. Since  
   \(L(P,f,lpha)\le S(P,f,lpha)\le U(P,f,lpha)\),  
   taking the supremum of lower sums gives:
   \[
   \sup_P L(P,f,lpha)\le A.
   \]
2. Similarly:
   \[
   A\le \inf_P U(P,f,lpha).
   \]
3. Letting the mesh shrink and using the convergence hypothesis shows:
   \[
   \inf_P U(P,f,lpha)-\sup_P L(P,f,lpha) < arepsilon.
   \]
4. Thus the upper and lower integrals coincide and equal \(A\).

## Additional Illustration
Consider the function:
\[
f(x)=
egin{cases}
1,& x\in\mathbb{Q},\
-1,& x
otin\mathbb{Q},
\end{cases}
\]
on \([0,1]\).  
- It is bounded but not Riemann integrable.  
- Lower sum = \(-1\), upper sum = \(1\).  
- Gap = \(2\), so no convergence.

But for \( |f|=1 \), both bounds = 1 → integrable.

This example demonstrates how RS sums detect integrability via shrinking the upper–lower gap.

## Summary
- RS sums are bounded between lower and upper RS sums.
- Convergence of RS sums characterizes integrability.
- The RS integral equals the common limit of all RS sums as mesh → 0.
- This fully connects RS sums with the foundational definition of the RS integral.
