# U02L05 – Sufficient Conditions for Integrability (Transcript)

## Learning Outcomes
- Understand integrability when the integrator is a step function.
- Prove that RS integrals with unit step integrators reduce to weighted sums.
- Apply comparison tests to show convergence of integrators.
- Use continuity of \(f\) at specified points to compute integrals.

## Introduction
This lecture focuses on integrals of the form  
\[
\int_a^b f\, dlpha,
\]
where the integrator \( lpha \) is a sum of weighted unit step functions. These integrators model discrete jumps. We show that the RS integral collapses to a discrete sum.

## Step Function Integrator
Given:
\[
lpha(x) = \sum_{n=1}^\infty c_n\, I(x - s_n),
\]
where  
- \(c_n \ge 0\),  
- \(\sum c_n\) convergent,  
- \(s_n \in (a,b)\),  
- \(I(x-s_n)\) is the unit step function:
  \[
  I(x-s_n) =
  egin{cases}
  0, & x \le s_n,\
  1, & x > s_n.
  \end{cases}
  \]

Since \(0 \le I(x-s_n) \le 1\), convergence of \(\sum c_n\) implies convergence of \(\sum c_n I(x-s_n)\).

Also \(I(x-s_n)\) is increasing in \(x\), hence so is \(lpha\).

## Main Result
\[
\int_a^b f\, dlpha
= \sum_{n=1}^\infty c_n\, f(s_n).
\]

### Proof Sketch
Let \(P\) be a partition. Then:
\[
\Delta lpha_i
= lpha(x_i)-lpha(x_{i-1})
= \sum_{n=1}^\infty c_n [I(x_i - s_n) - I(x_{i-1}-s_n)].
\]

But  
\[
I(x_i - s_n) - I(x_{i-1}-s_n)
=
egin{cases}
1, & s_n \in (x_{i-1},x_i],\
0, & 	ext{otherwise}.
\end{cases}
\]

Thus the RS sum becomes:
\[
\sum_i f(t_i)\Deltalpha_i
= \sum_{n=1}^\infty c_n f(s_n),
\]
independent of the partition.  
Therefore:
\[
\int_a^b f\, dlpha = \sum_{n=1}^\infty c_n f(s_n).
\]

## Example: Single Jump
If
\[
lpha(x) = I(x-s), \quad a < s < b,
\]
and \(f\) is bounded and continuous at \(s\), then:
\[
\int_a^b f\, dlpha = f(s).
\]

**Explanation:**  
Partition at \(s\):  
- Only the subinterval containing \(s\) contributes.  
- Upper sum = \(M_2\), lower sum = \(m_2\).  
- Continuity forces \(M_2, m_2 	o f(s)\).  
Thus the integral equals \(f(s)\).

## Summary
- Step integrators produce discrete RS integrals.
- Convergence of \(\sum c_n\) ensures \( lpha\) is monotone and finite.
- RS sums collapse to weighted point evaluations.
- This generalizes the idea that RS integrals capture “jump contributions.”
