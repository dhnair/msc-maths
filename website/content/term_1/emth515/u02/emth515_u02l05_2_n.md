# U02L05 – Sufficient Conditions for Integrability (Notes)

## Key Results
### 1. Unit Step-Based Integrator
If
\[
lpha(x) = \sum_{n=1}^\infty c_n\, I(x-s_n),
\]
with
- \(c_n \ge 0\),
- \(\sum c_n\) convergent,
- \(s_n \in (a,b)\),

then
\[
\int_a^b f\, dlpha = \sum_{n=1}^\infty c_n f(s_n).
\]

### 2. Properties Used
- \(I(x-s_n)\) is increasing.
- Convergence of \(\sum c_n\) guarantees \(lpha\) bounded and monotone.
- On each interval, RS increments pick out exactly those \(s_n\) inside.

### 3. Example (Single Jump)
With
\[
lpha(x) = I(x-s),
\]
and \(f\) continuous at \(s\):
\[
\int_a^b f\, dlpha = f(s).
\]

## Important Formulas
- Riemann–Stieltjes sum:
  \[
  S(P,f,lpha) = \sum f(t_i)\Deltalpha_i.
  \]
- Step increment:
  \[
  \Deltalpha_i = \sum c_n\, [I(x_i-s_n) - I(x_{i-1}-s_n)].
  \]

## Summary
- Step integrators act like “discrete selectors.”
- RS integral becomes a weighted sum of function evaluations.
- Continuity at jump points ensures exact value extraction.
