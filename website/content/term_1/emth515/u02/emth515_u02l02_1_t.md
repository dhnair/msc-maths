# U02L02 - Boundedness and Integrability Conditions (Transcript)

## Learning Outcomes
- Understand the fifth and sixth fundamental properties of the Riemann-Stieltjes integral.
- Show that $|f|$ is Riemann-Stieltjes integrable whenever $f$ is.
- Establish the inequality $|\int_a^b f\, d\alpha| \le \int_a^b |f|\, d\alpha$.
- Learn how integrability behaves on subintervals and how the integral splits at interior points.

## Introduction
This lecture continues the study of algebraic and structural properties of the Riemann-Stieltjes integral. Two major themes appear: integrability of $|f|$, and the additivity of the integral across subintervals.

## Definitions
- $U(P,f,\alpha)$ and $L(P,f,\alpha)$ are the upper and lower RS sums.
- $M_i,m_i$: supremum and infimum of $f$ on $[x_{i-1},x_i]$.
- $M_i',m_i'$: supremum and infimum of $|f|$ on the same subinterval.

## Main Development

### 1. Property: If $f \in R(\alpha)$, then $|f| \in R(\alpha)$
Given $f$ is RS-integrable:
- There exists a partition $P$ with
  $$
  U(P,f,\alpha) - L(P,f,\alpha) < \epsilon.
  $$
- For any $t_1,t_2$ in a subinterval:
  $$
  ||f(t_1)| - |f(t_2)|| \le |f(t_1) - f(t_2)|.
  $$
- Thus:
  $$
  M_i' - m_i' \le M_i - m_i.
  $$
- Summing yields:
  $$
  U(P,|f|,\alpha) - L(P,|f|,\alpha)
  < \epsilon.
  $$
Conclusion: $|f|$ is RS-integrable.

### 2. Property: $| \int_a^b f\, d\alpha | \le \int_a^b |f|\, d\alpha$
Since $f \in R(\alpha)$ implies $-f \in R(\alpha)$ and $|f| \in R(\alpha)$:
- From pointwise inequalities:
  $$
  -|f| \le f \le |f|.
  $$
- Apply integrals:
  $$
  -\int_a^b |f| d\alpha \le \int_a^b f d\alpha \le \int_a^b |f| d\alpha.
  $$
- Combine:
  $$
  |\int_a^b f d\alpha| \le \int_a^b |f| d\alpha.
  $$

### 3. Integrability on subintervals and additivity of the integral
If $f \in R(\alpha)$ on $[a,b]$ and $c$ is an interior point $(a<c<b)$:

#### Step 1: Refinement argument
- Take a partition $P$ with:
  $$
  U(P,f,\alpha) - L(P,f,\alpha) < \epsilon.
  $$
- Insert $c$ into $P$ to form $P^*$.
- Properties of refinements guarantee:
  $$
  U(P^*,f,\alpha) - L(P^*,f,\alpha) < \epsilon.
  $$

#### Step 2: Splitting into two partitions
Let $P_1$ and $P_2$ be the induced partitions on $[a,c]$ and $[c,b]$.

Show:
- $f \in R(\alpha)$ on both subintervals.
- Inequality:
  $$
  U(P_1,f,\alpha) + U(P_2,f,\alpha)
  = U(P^*,f,\alpha).
  $$
- Same relationship holds for lower sums.

#### Step 3: Combine upper and lower bounds
Derivations yield:
  $$
  \int_a^b f\, d\alpha
  = \int_a^c f\, d\alpha + \int_c^b f\, d\alpha.
  $$

## Summary
This lecture proved:
- $|f|$ is RS-integrable whenever $f$ is.
- Absolute value inequality for RS integrals.
- Integrability holds on subintervals and integrals split at interior points.
These results extend the basic structural behavior of RS integrals and allow decomposition of integrals across partitions.
