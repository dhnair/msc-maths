# U02L06 – Functions of Bounded Variation (Transcript)

## Learning Outcomes
- Understand the relationship between Riemann–Stieltjes integral and the classical Riemann integral.
- Prove that if \( lpha' \) is Riemann integrable, then \( f \in R(lpha) \iff flpha' \) is Riemann integrable.
- Use the mean value theorem to connect increments of \( lpha \) with values of \( lpha' \).

## Statement of Theorem
Let:
- \( f \) be bounded on \([a,b]\),
- \( lpha \) be monotonically increasing on \([a,b]\),
- \( lpha' \) be Riemann integrable on \([a,b]\).

Then:

1. \( f \in R(lpha) \) if and only if \( flpha' \in R \) (Riemann integrable).
2. The integrals satisfy:
\[
\int_a^b f\, dlpha = \int_a^b f(x)\, lpha'(x)\, dx.
\]

## Proof Sketch

### 1. Using Lagrange Mean Value Theorem
Since \( lpha' \) is Riemann integrable, \( lpha \) is differentiable with continuous derivative on each subinterval \([x_{i-1},x_i]\).  
Apply the Mean Value Theorem:

\[

= lpha'(t_i),
\]

so

\[
\Delta lpha_i = lpha'(t_i)\, \Delta x_i.
\]

### 2. Compare RS sum and Riemann sum
Riemann–Stieltjes sum:
\[
S(P,f,lpha) = \sum f(t_i)\Deltalpha_i
= \sum f(t_i)lpha'(t_i)\Delta x_i
= S(P,flpha').
\]

Thus for a given partition:

\[
|S(P,f,lpha) - S(P,flpha')| < k\,arepsilon,
\]

where \(k\) bounds \(|f|\).

### 3. Use integrability of \(lpha'\)
Since:
\[
U(P,lpha') - L(P,lpha') < arepsilon
\]
for sufficiently fine partitions, the same holds for the RS sums through the above expressions.

Thus:
\[
\left| \int_a^b f\, dlpha - \int_a^b flpha' \, dx ight| < karepsilon.
\]

As \( arepsilon 	o 0 \), the equality of the two integrals follows.

## Summary
- When \( lpha' \) exists and is Riemann integrable, Riemann–Stieltjes integration reduces to classical Riemann integration with weight \( lpha' \).
- The key tool is expressing \( \Deltalpha_i = lpha'(t_i)\Delta x_i \) using the Mean Value Theorem.
- The equivalence \( f\in R(lpha) \iff flpha'\in R \) follows from comparing corresponding sums.
