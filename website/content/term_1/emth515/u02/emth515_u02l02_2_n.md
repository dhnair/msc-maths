# U02L02 - Boundedness and Integrability Conditions (Notes)

## Key Points
- If $f \in R(\alpha)$ then $|f| \in R(\alpha)$.
- Absolute inequality: $|\int_a^b f d\alpha| \le \int_a^b |f| d\alpha$.
- Integrability on $[a,c]$ and $[c,b]$ follows from integrability on $[a,b]$.
- Additivity of the RS integral across subintervals.

## Definitions and Notation
- $M_i,m_i$: bounds of $f$ on each subinterval.
- $M_i',m_i'$: bounds of $|f|$.
- $P, P^*$: partition and its refinement by inserting $c$.

## Core Results
- $U(P,|f|,\alpha) - L(P,|f|,\alpha) \le U(P,f,\alpha) - L(P,f,\alpha)$.
- If $f \in R(\alpha)$ then $|f| \in R(\alpha)$.
- Pointwise inequality $-|f| \le f \le |f|$ leads to the integral inequality.
- Additivity:
  $$
  \int_a^b f d\alpha = \int_a^c f d\alpha + \int_c^b f d\alpha.
  $$

## Important Derivations
- Difference of bounds:
  $$
  M_i' - m_i' \le M_i - m_i.
  $$
- Integral splitting derived from:
  - Refinement $P^*$,
  - Equality of sums:
    $U(P^*,f,\alpha) = U(P_1,f,\alpha) + U(P_2,f,\alpha)$.

## Relationships and Interpretations
- Absolute integrability property mirrors classical Riemann theory.
- Splitting property enables piecewise evaluation of RS integrals.

## Summary
- $|f|$ integrability, absolute inequality, and subinterval additivity complete another group of structural properties of RS integrals.
