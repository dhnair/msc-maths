# U01L04 - Conditions Ensuring Riemann-Stieltjes Integrability (Notes)

## Key Points
- Recap of Riemann-Stieltjes sums and their inequalities.
- Refinement decreases upper sums and increases lower sums.
- Lower integral always $\le$ upper integral.
- Integrability: $U(P) - L(P) < \epsilon$.

## Definitions and Notation
- $m_i$: $\inf f$ on $[x_{i-1},x_i]$.
- $M_i$: $\sup f$ on $[x_{i-1},x_i]$.
- $\Delta\alpha_i = \alpha(x_i) - \alpha(x_{i-1})$.
- Lower sum: $L(P,f,\alpha)$.
- Upper sum: $U(P,f,\alpha)$.

## Core Results
- Always:
  $$
  L(P,f,\alpha) \le U(P,f,\alpha).
  $$
- Lower integral:
  $$
  \underline{\int} = \sup L(P,f,\alpha).
  $$
- Upper integral:
  $$
  \overline{\int} = \inf U(P,f,\alpha).
  $$
- Refinement:
  - $U(P^*) \le U(P)$,
  - $L(P^*) \ge L(P)$.

## Important Derivations
- From integrability:
  $$
  U(P,f,\alpha) - L(P,f,\alpha) < \epsilon.
  $$
- Convergence of sums:
  $$
  | \sum f(t_i)\Delta\alpha_i - \int f\, d\alpha | < \epsilon.
  $$

## Relationships and Interpretations
- Lower sums approach the integral from below.
- Upper sums approach the integral from above.
- Refinements tighten both bounds.

## Summary
- L04 recaps all essential relationships supporting integrability.
- Sets stage for later theorems and numerical examples.
