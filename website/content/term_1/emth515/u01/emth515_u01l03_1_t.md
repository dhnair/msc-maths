# U01L03 - Convergence of Sums and Integrability Criteria (Transcript)

## Learning Outcomes
- Understand the necessary and sufficient condition for Riemann-Stieltjes integrability.
- Analyze the behavior of upper and lower sums under integrability assumptions.
- Follow the proof that integrability is equivalent to making upper and lower sums arbitrarily close.

## Introduction
This lecture builds on earlier results concerning upper and lower Riemann-Stieltjes sums. Previously, we established how refinement affects these sums. Today, we study the fundamental criterion for the existence of the Riemann-Stieltjes integral and prove the equivalence between integrability and the ability to make upper and lower sums arbitrarily close.

## Definitions
- $f$: bounded function on $[a,b]$.
- $\alpha$: bounded and monotonically increasing function on $[a,b]$.
- $P$: a partition of $[a,b]$.
- $L(P,f,\alpha)$: lower Riemann-Stieltjes sum.
- $U(P,f,\alpha)$: upper Riemann-Stieltjes sum.
- $f \in R(\alpha)$: $f$ is Riemann-Stieltjes integrable w.r.t. $\alpha$.

## Main Development

### 1. Statement of the Theorem
Let $f$ and $\alpha$ be bounded on $[a,b]$ with $\alpha$ monotonically increasing. Then:
$$
f \in R(\alpha) \quad \text{iff} \quad \forall \epsilon > 0,\ \exists P \text{ such that } U(P,f,\alpha) - L(P,f,\alpha) < \epsilon.
$$

### 2. First Direction: $f \in R(\alpha) \Rightarrow$ sums can be made arbitrarily close
Assume $f \in R(\alpha)$, so the upper and lower integrals coincide:
$$
\underline{\int_a^b} f\,d\alpha = \overline{\int_a^b} f\,d\alpha = \int_a^b f\,d\alpha.
$$

Let $\epsilon > 0$. Since
$$
\overline{\int_a^b} f\,d\alpha = \inf U(P,f,\alpha),
$$
the number
$$
\int_a^b f\,d\alpha + \frac{\epsilon}{2}
$$
cannot be a lower bound for the upper sums. Thus, there exists a partition $P_1$ such that:
$$
U(P_1,f,\alpha) < \int_a^b f\,d\alpha + \frac{\epsilon}{2}.
$$

Similarly, since
$$
\underline{\int_a^b} f\,d\alpha = \sup L(P,f,\alpha),
$$
there exists a partition $P_2$ such that:
$$
L(P_2,f,\alpha) > \int_a^b f\,d\alpha - \frac{\epsilon}{2}.
$$

Let $P^* = P_1 \cup P_2$ be the common refinement. Using monotonicity:
- $U(P^*) \le U(P_1)$,
- $L(P^*) \ge L(P_2)$.

Thus,
$$
U(P^*) - L(P^*) < \left( \int_a^b f\,d\alpha + \frac{\epsilon}{2} \right) - \left( \int_a^b f\,d\alpha - \frac{\epsilon}{2} \right) = \epsilon.
$$

### 3. Second Direction: Upper and Lower Sums Arbitrarily Close $\Rightarrow f \in R(\alpha)$
Assume for every $\epsilon>0$ there exists $P$ such that:
$$
U(P,f,\alpha) - L(P,f,\alpha) < \epsilon.
$$

Then:
- $L(P,f,\alpha)$ is always a lower bound for the upper integral.
- $U(P,f,\alpha)$ is always an upper bound for the lower integral.

Since these two numbers differ by less than any $\epsilon$, the lower and upper integrals must coincide. Therefore:
$$
f \in R(\alpha).
$$

### 4. Final Inequality for Riemann-Stieltjes Integrable Functions
If $f \in R(\alpha)$, then for any $\epsilon > 0$ and corresponding partition:
$$
| \sum_{i=1}^n f(t_i)\Delta\alpha_i - \int_a^b f\,d\alpha | < \epsilon.
$$

## Summary
- Integrability is equivalent to making upper and lower sums arbitrarily close.
- The proof uses the fact that the integral equals both the supremum of lower sums and the infimum of upper sums.
- Common refinement ensures estimates work simultaneously.
