# U01L02 - Refinement of Partitions: Monotonicity of Upper and Lower Sums (Notes)

## Key Points
- Refining a partition changes intervals but keeps endpoints from the original partition.
- Upper Riemann-Stieltjes sums decrease under refinement.
- Lower Riemann-Stieltjes sums increase under refinement.
- Lower integral is always less than or equal to the upper integral.

## Definitions and Notation
- $P$: a partition of $[a,b]$.
- $P^*$: a refinement of $P$.
- $m_i = \inf f(x)$ on $[x_{i-1}, x_i]$.
- $M_i = \sup f(x)$ on $[x_{i-1}, x_i]$.
- $\Delta\alpha_i = \alpha(x_i) - \alpha(x_{i-1})$.
- Lower sum: $L(P,f,\alpha) = \sum m_i \Delta\alpha_i$.
- Upper sum: $U(P,f,\alpha) = \sum M_i \Delta\alpha_i$.

## Core Results
- Refinement property:
  - $U(P^*,f,\alpha) \le U(P,f,\alpha)$.
  - $L(P^*,f,\alpha) \ge L(P,f,\alpha)$.
- Inequality between integrals:
  - $\underline{\int_a^b} f \, d\alpha \le \overline{\int_a^b} f \, d\alpha$.

## Important Derivations
- For the refined subintervals:
  - $W_1 \le M_j$, $W_2 \le M_j$.
  - $w_1 \ge m_j$, $w_2 \ge m_j$.
- Upper sum difference:
  $$
  U(P^*) - U(P) = W_1[\alpha(y)-\alpha(x_{j-1})] + W_2[\alpha(x_j)-\alpha(y)] - M_j \Delta\alpha_j \le 0.
  $$
- Lower sum difference:
  $$
  L(P^*) - L(P) = w_1[\alpha(y)-\alpha(x_{j-1})] + w_2[\alpha(x_j)-\alpha(y)] - m_j \Delta\alpha_j \ge 0.
  $$

## Relationships and Interpretations
- Refinement narrows intervals, making extrema closer to actual function values.
- Hence upper sums drop and lower sums rise.
- Common refinement $P^*$ links any two partitions:
  - $L(P_1) \le L(P^*) \le U(P^*) \le U(P_2)$.
- This produces the lower-vs-upper integral inequality.

## Summary
- Refinement monotonicity is fundamental for integrability.
- Lower sums approach the lower integral from below.
- Upper sums approach the upper integral from above.
- Always:
  $$
  \underline{\int_a^b} f \, d\alpha \le \overline{\int_a^b} f \, d\alpha.
  $$
