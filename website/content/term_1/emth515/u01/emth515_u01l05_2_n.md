# U01L05 - Composition Theorem for Riemann-Stieltjes Integrability (Notes)

## Key Points
- If $f \in R(\alpha)$ and $\phi$ is continuous on the range of $f$, then $H = \phi(f)$ is also integrable.
- Uniform continuity of $\phi$ ensures control over oscillation.
- Oscillation of $H$ mirrors oscillation of $f$ on small intervals.
- Upper and lower sums for $H$ can be made arbitrarily close.

## Definitions and Notation
- $m \le f(x) \le M$: bounds on $f$.
- $\phi: [m,M] \to \mathbb{R}$: continuous map.
- $H(x) = \phi(f(x))$.
- $m_i, M_i$: infimum and supremum of $f$ on $[x_{i-1},x_i]$.
- $m_i', M_i'$: infimum and supremum of $H$ on $[x_{i-1},x_i]$.

## Core Results
- **Theorem:**  
  If $f \in R(\alpha)$ and $\phi$ is continuous on $[m,M]$, then  
  $$
  \phi(f) \in R(\alpha).
  $$

## Important Derivations
- Uniform continuity:  
  $$|s - t| < \delta \Rightarrow |\phi(s) - \phi(t)| < \epsilon.$$
- Since $f \in R(\alpha)$, choose $P$ s.t.  
  $$U(P,f,\alpha) - L(P,f,\alpha) < \delta^2.$$
- Then  
  $$M_i - m_i < \delta.$$
- Hence  
  $$M_i' - m_i' < \epsilon.$$
- Final estimate:  
  $$
  U(P,H,\alpha) - L(P,H,\alpha)
  < \epsilon [\alpha(b) - \alpha(a)].
  $$

## Relationships and Interpretations
- Oscillation of $H$ is governed by oscillation of $f$.
- Integrability is preserved under continuous transformations.

## Summary
- Uniform continuity of $\phi$ is the key tool.
- Small variation in $f$ ensures small variation in $H$.
- Therefore $\phi(f)$ is Riemann-Stieltjes integrable.
