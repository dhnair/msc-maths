# U01L05 - Composition Theorem for Riemann-Stieltjes Integrability (Transcript)

## Learning Outcomes
- Understand how continuity of a function applied to $f$ affects integrability.
- Learn the sufficient condition ensuring that $\phi(f)$ is Riemann-Stieltjes integrable.
- Follow the proof that uniform continuity of $\phi$ controls oscillation on subintervals.
- See how upper and lower sums behave when a composition $H = \phi(f)$ is formed.

## Introduction
This lecture develops a key theorem related to composite functions in the context of the Riemann-Stieltjes integral. The focus is to show that if $f$ is Riemann-Stieltjes integrable with respect to $\alpha$, and $\phi$ is continuous on the range of $f$, then the composed function $H = \phi(f)$ is also Riemann-Stieltjes integrable with respect to $\alpha$.

## Definitions
- $f$: a bounded function on $[a,b]$.
- $\alpha$: a monotonically increasing function on $[a,b]$.
- $m \le f(x) \le M$: closed interval containing all values of $f$.
- $\phi: [m,M] \to \mathbb{R}$: continuous function.
- $H(x) = \phi(f(x))$.
- $M_i$: supremum of $f$ on $[x_{i-1},x_i]$.
- $m_i$: infimum of $f$ on $[x_{i-1},x_i]$.
- $M_i'$: supremum of $H$ on $[x_{i-1},x_i]$.
- $m_i'$: infimum of $H$ on $[x_{i-1},x_i]$.

## Main Development

### 1. Statement of the theorem
Let $f \in R(\alpha)$ on $[a,b]$ and suppose  
$$
m \le f(x) \le M \quad 	ext{for all } x \in [a,b].
$$  
Let $\phi: [m,M] \to \mathbb{R}$ be continuous.  
Define  
$$
H(x) = \phi(f(x)).
$$  
Then  
$$
H \in R(\alpha).
$$

### 2. Uniform continuity of $\phi$
Since $\phi$ is continuous on the compact interval $[m,M]$, it is uniformly continuous.  
Thus, for any $\epsilon > 0$, there exists $\delta > 0$ such that:
$$
|\phi(s) - \phi(t)| < \epsilon \quad 	ext{whenever } |s - t| < \delta.
$$  
This is the key tool that will allow control over the oscillation of $H$.

### 3. Using integrability of $f$
Since $f \in R(\alpha)$, for given $\delta^2 > 0$ there exists a partition $P$ such that:
$$
U(P,f,\alpha) - L(P,f,\alpha) < \delta^2.
$$  
This implies that on each subinterval $[x_{i-1},x_i]$ we have:
$$
M_i - m_i < \delta.
$$

### 4. Effect on the composite function $H = \phi(f)$
On each interval $[x_{i-1},x_i]$, we have:
$$
|f(x) - f(y)| < \delta \; \Rightarrow \; |\phi(f(x)) - \phi(f(y))| < \epsilon.
$$  
Thus the oscillation of $H$ on each subinterval satisfies:
$$
M_i' - m_i' < \epsilon.
$$  

### 5. Bounding the Riemann-Stieltjes sums
Compute:
$$
U(P,H,\alpha) - L(P,H,\alpha)
= \sum_{i=1}^n (M_i' - m_i') \Delta\alpha_i.
$$  

Since $(M_i' - m_i') < \epsilon$ and $\Delta\alpha_i \ge 0$, we obtain:
$$
U(P,H,\alpha) - L(P,H,\alpha)
< \epsilon \sum_{i=1}^n \Delta\alpha_i
= \epsilon [\alpha(b) - \alpha(a)].
$$  

Because $\epsilon$ is arbitrary, this establishes integrability:
$$
H \in R(\alpha).
$$

## Summary
- The composition theorem states: if $f \in R(\alpha)$ and $\phi$ is continuous on the range of $f$, then $\phi(f)$ is also Riemann-Stieltjes integrable.
- The proof relies on uniform continuity of $\phi$.
- Small oscillation of $f$ on each subinterval forces small oscillation of $H$.
- Upper and lower sums of $H$ can be made arbitrarily close; therefore $H \in R(\alpha)$.
