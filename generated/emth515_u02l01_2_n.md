# U02L01 - Basic Properties of RS Sums (Notes)

## Key Points
- Scalar multiples of integrable functions remain integrable.
- Sum of two integrable functions is integrable.
- Square of an integrable function is integrable.
- Product of two integrable functions is integrable.

## Definitions and Notation
- $U(P,f,\alpha)$, $L(P,f,\alpha)$: upper and lower RS sums.
- $M_i,m_i$: supremum and infimum of $f$ on $[x_{i-1},x_i]$.

## Core Results
- $cf \in R(\alpha)$ for any real $c$.
- $(f_1+f_2) \in R(\alpha)$ and integrals add.
- $f^2 \in R(\alpha)$.
- $fg \in R(\alpha)$ using $(f+g)^2-(f-g)^2 = 4fg$.

## Important Derivations
- For $c>0$:  
  $U(P,cf,\alpha)-L(P,cf,\alpha)=c[U(P,f,\alpha)-L(P,f,\alpha)]$.
- For $f^2$:  
  $|f^2(t_1)-f^2(t_2)| \le 2k |f(t_1)-f(t_2)|$.
- For product:  
  $4fg = (f+g)^2 - (f-g)^2$.

## Relationships and Interpretations
- Integrability is preserved under linear operations.
- Square and product follow from boundedness combined with previous properties.

## Summary
- Unit 2 begins with algebraic closure properties for Riemann-Stieltjes integrable functions using upper and lower sums.
