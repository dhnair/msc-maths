# U02L05 - Sufficient Conditions for Integrability (Notes)

## Key Points
- Integrator α formed as α(x)=∑ c_n I(x-s_n), c_n≥0, ∑ c_n convergent.
- f continuous ⇒ ∫ f dα = ∑ c_n f(s_n).
- Tail of α contributes arbitrarily small amount.
- Change-of-variables theorem for RS integral.

## Definitions and Notation
- I(x-s): unit step.
- α₁ finite-sum part, α₂ tail.
- β(y)=α(φ(y)), g(y)=f(φ(y)).

## Core Results
- α monotonic and convergent.
- ∫ f dα₁ = ∑_{n=1}^M c_n f(s_n).
- |∫ f dα₂| ≤ K ∑_{n>M} c_n.
- ∫ f dα = ∑_{n=1}^\infty c_n f(s_n).
- Change-of-variable formula: ∫ f dα = ∫ g dβ.

## Important Derivations
- Comparison test for α.
- Bounding the tail integral using K(α₂(b)-α₂(a)).
- Partition correspondence under φ.

## Relationships
- Jump integrators reduce RS integral to pointwise evaluation.
- Transformation invariance under increasing continuous φ.

## Summary
Infinite jump integrators produce series-valued integrals, and RS integrals remain stable under monotone substitutions.
