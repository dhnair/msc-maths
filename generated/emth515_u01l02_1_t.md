# U01L02 - Refinement of Partitions: Monotonicity of Upper and Lower Sums (Transcript)

## Learning Outcomes
- Understand how refinement of a partition affects Riemann-Stieltjes upper and lower sums.
- Prove that upper sums decrease under refinement.
- Prove that lower sums increase under refinement.
- Establish that the lower Riemann-Stieltjes integral is always less than or equal to the upper integral.

## Introduction
This lecture continues the study of the Riemann-Stieltjes integral begun in Lecture U01L01. The focus here is on how partition refinement influences the upper and lower Riemann-Stieltjes sums. These properties form the foundation for showing that the lower integral never exceeds the upper integral.

## Definitions
We work with:
- A bounded function $f$ on $[a,b]$.
- A monotonically increasing function $\alpha$ on $[a,b]$.
- A partition $P = \{x_0, x_1, \ldots, x_n\}$ of the interval.
- Refinement: a partition $P^*$ is a refinement of $P$ if it contains all points of $P$ and possibly more.

For any subinterval $[x_{i-1}, x_i]$:
- $m_i = \inf f(x)$ on the interval.
- $M_i = \sup f(x)$ on the interval.
- $\Delta\alpha_i = \alpha(x_i) - \alpha(x_{i-1})$.

Lower and upper Riemann-Stieltjes sums:
- $L(P,f,\alpha) = \sum_{i=1}^n m_i \Delta\alpha_i$.
- $U(P,f,\alpha) = \sum_{i=1}^n M_i \Delta\alpha_i$.

## Main Development

### 1. Effect of Refinement on Upper Sums
Let $P$ be a partition of $[a,b]$:
$$P = \{x_0, x_1, \ldots, x_{j-1}, x_j, \ldots, x_n\}.$$

Let $P^*$ be a refinement obtained by inserting a new point $y$ in the subinterval $[x_{j-1}, x_j]$:
$$P^* = \{x_0, x_1, \ldots, x_{j-1}, y, x_j, \ldots, x_n\}.$$

Define:
- $w_1, W_1$ as the infimum and supremum of $f$ on $[x_{j-1}, y]$.
- $w_2, W_2$ as the infimum and supremum of $f$ on $[y, x_j]$.

Since both subintervals lie within $[x_{j-1}, x_j]$:
- $W_1 \le M_j$, $W_2 \le M_j$.
- $w_1 \ge m_j$, $w_2 \ge m_j$.

Compute:
$$U(P^*,f,\alpha) - U(P,f,\alpha) = W_1[\alpha(y)-\alpha(x_{j-1})] + W_2[\alpha(x_j)-\alpha(y)] - M_j\Delta\alpha_j.$$

Using $W_1,W_2 \le M_j$ and $\Delta\alpha_j = \alpha(x_j) - \alpha(x_{j-1})$:
$$U(P^*,f,\alpha) - U(P,f,\alpha) \le 0.$$
Therefore,
$$U(P^*,f,\alpha) \le U(P,f,\alpha).$$

### 2. Effect of Refinement on Lower Sums
Similarly, compute:
$$L(P^*,f,\alpha) - L(P,f,\alpha) = w_1[\alpha(y)-\alpha(x_{j-1})] + w_2[\alpha(x_j)-\alpha(y)] - m_j\Delta\alpha_j.$$

Since $w_1,w_2 \ge m_j$:
$$L(P^*,f,\alpha) - L(P,f,\alpha) \ge 0.$$
Thus,
$$L(P^*,f,\alpha) \ge L(P,f,\alpha).$$

### 3. Comparing Lower and Upper Integrals
Let $P_1$ and $P_2$ be any two partitions of $[a,b]$, and let $P^* = P_1 \cup P_2$ be their common refinement.

Using monotonicity:
- $L(P_1) \le L(P^*) \le U(P^*) \le U(P_2)$.

Taking supremum over all $P_1$ and infimum over all $P_2$:
$$\underline{\int_a^b} f \, d\alpha \le \overline{\int_a^b} f \, d\alpha.$$

## Summary
- Refining a partition makes the upper sum smaller and the lower sum larger.
- For any two partitions, the lower sum of one is always less than or equal to the upper sum of the other.
- This implies the fundamental inequality between the lower and upper Riemann-Stieltjes integrals.

## Glossary
- Refinement: A partition containing all points of another partition.
- Upper sum: Supremum-based Riemann-Stieltjes sum.
- Lower sum: Infimum-based Riemann-Stieltjes sum.
- Riemann-Stieltjes sum: A sum weighted by $\Delta\alpha_i$.
