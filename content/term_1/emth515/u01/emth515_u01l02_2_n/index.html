U01L02 - Refinement of Partitions: Monotonicity of Upper and Lower Sums (Notes)

Key Points​

  • Refining a partition changes intervals but keeps endpoints from the original partition.
  • Upper Riemann-Stieltjes sums decrease under refinement.
  • Lower Riemann-Stieltjes sums increase under refinement.
  • Lower integral is always less than or equal to the upper integral.

Definitions and Notation​

  • PPP: a partition of [a,b][a,b][a,b].
  • P∗P^*P∗: a refinement of PPP.
  • mi=inf⁡f(x)m_i = \inf f(x)mi​=inff(x) on [xi−1,xi][x_{i-1}, x_i][xi−1​,xi​].
  • Mi=sup⁡f(x)M_i = \sup f(x)Mi​=supf(x) on [xi−1,xi][x_{i-1}, x_i][xi−1​,xi​].
  • Δαi=α(xi)−α(xi−1)\Delta\alpha_i = \alpha(x_i) - \alpha(x_{i-1})Δαi​=α(xi​)−α(xi−1​).
  • Lower sum: L(P,f,α)=∑miΔαiL(P,f,\alpha) = \sum m_i \Delta\alpha_iL(P,f,α)=∑mi​Δαi​.
  • Upper sum: U(P,f,α)=∑MiΔαiU(P,f,\alpha) = \sum M_i \Delta\alpha_iU(P,f,α)=∑Mi​Δαi​.

Core Results​

  • Refinement property:
    • U(P∗,f,α)≤U(P,f,α)U(P^*,f,\alpha) \le U(P,f,\alpha)U(P∗,f,α)≤U(P,f,α).
    • L(P∗,f,α)≥L(P,f,α)L(P^*,f,\alpha) \ge L(P,f,\alpha)L(P∗,f,α)≥L(P,f,α).
  • Inequality between integrals:
    • ∫ab‾f dα≤∫ab‾f dα\underline{\int_a^b} f \, d\alpha \le \overline{\int_a^b} f \, d\alpha∫ab​​fdα≤∫ab​​fdα.

Important Derivations​

  • For the refined subintervals:
    • W1≤MjW_1 \le M_jW1​≤Mj​, W2≤MjW_2 \le M_jW2​≤Mj​.
    • w1≥mjw_1 \ge m_jw1​≥mj​, w2≥mjw_2 \ge m_jw2​≥mj​.
  • Upper sum difference: U(P∗)−U(P)=W1[α(y)−α(xj−1)]+W2[α(xj)−α(y)]−MjΔαj≤0.U(P^*) - U(P) = W_1[\alpha(y)-\alpha(x_{j-1})] + W_2[\alpha(x_j)-\alpha(y)] - M_j \Delta\alpha_j \le 0.U(P∗)−U(P)=W1​[α(y)−α(xj−1​)]+W2​[α(xj​)−α(y)]−Mj​Δαj​≤0.
  • Lower sum difference: L(P∗)−L(P)=w1[α(y)−α(xj−1)]+w2[α(xj)−α(y)]−mjΔαj≥0.L(P^*) - L(P) = w_1[\alpha(y)-\alpha(x_{j-1})] + w_2[\alpha(x_j)-\alpha(y)] - m_j \Delta\alpha_j \ge 0.L(P∗)−L(P)=w1​[α(y)−α(xj−1​)]+w2​[α(xj​)−α(y)]−mj​Δαj​≥0.

Relationships and Interpretations​

  • Refinement narrows intervals, making extrema closer to actual function values.
  • Hence upper sums drop and lower sums rise.
  • Common refinement P∗P^*P∗ links any two partitions:
    • L(P1)≤L(P∗)≤U(P∗)≤U(P2)L(P_1) \le L(P^*) \le U(P^*) \le U(P_2)L(P1​)≤L(P∗)≤U(P∗)≤U(P2​).
  • This produces the lower-vs-upper integral inequality.

Summary​

  • Refinement monotonicity is fundamental for integrability.
  • Lower sums approach the lower integral from below.
  • Upper sums approach the upper integral from above.
