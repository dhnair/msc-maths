U01L04 - Conditions Ensuring Riemann-Stieltjes Integrability (Transcript)

Learning Outcomes​

  • Recap essential concepts behind Riemann-Stieltjes sums.
  • Understand key relations between upper and lower sums and their integrals.
  • Review refinement properties.
  • Learn strong conditions that guarantee integrability.

Introduction​

This lecture begins with a brief recap of previously established results on Riemann-Stieltjes sums, upper and lower integrals, and the effect of refinement. The discussion then moves to further theorems that support the characterization of Riemann-Stieltjes integrable functions.

Definitions​

  • Lower sum:
    L(P,f,α)=∑i=1nmiΔαi,L(P,f,\alpha) = \sum_{i=1}^n m_i \Delta\alpha_i,L(P,f,α)=∑i=1n​mi​Δαi​, where mi=inf⁡f(x)m_i = \inf f(x)mi​=inff(x) on [xi−1,xi][x_{i-1},x_i][xi−1​,xi​].

  • Upper sum:
    U(P,f,α)=∑i=1nMiΔαi,U(P,f,\alpha) = \sum_{i=1}^n M_i \Delta\alpha_i,U(P,f,α)=∑i=1n​Mi​Δαi​, where Mi=sup⁡f(x)M_i = \sup f(x)Mi​=supf(x) on [xi−1,xi][x_{i-1},x_i][xi−1​,xi​].

  • Lower integral:

Main Development​

1. Review of earlier results​

  • Always:

    L(P,f,α)≤U(P,f,α).L(P,f,\alpha) \le U(P,f,\alpha).L(P,f,α)≤U(P,f,α).

  • Lower integral is always less than or equal to upper integral:

    ∫ab‾f dα≤∫ab‾f dα.\underline{\int_a^b} f\, d\alpha \le \overline{\int_a^b} f\, d\alpha.∫ab​​fdα≤∫ab​​fdα.

  • Refinement rule:

    • If P∗P^*P∗ refines PPP, then: U(P∗,f,α)≤U(P,f,α), L(P∗,f,α)≥L(P,f,α).U(P^*,f,\alpha) \le U(P,f,\alpha), \ L(P^*,f,\alpha) \ge L(P,f,\alpha).U(P∗,f,α)≤U(P,f,α), L(P∗,f,α)≥L(P,f,α).

  • Riemann-Stieltjes integrability condition:

    f∈R(α)  ⟺  U(P,f,α)−L(P,f,α)<ϵ.f \in R(\alpha) \iff U(P,f,\alpha) - L(P,f,\alpha) < \epsilon.f∈R(α)⟺U(P,f,α)−L(P,f,α)<ϵ.

Summary​

  • All major relationships between upper sums, lower sums, and integrals were recalled.
  • Refinement properties ensure monotonic behavior of sums.
  • Integrability is equivalent to squeezing upper and lower sums arbitrarily close.
